the goal of this map

this map is dedicated to Workman Arts (WA).


we understand WA as a non-profit art organisation which aims at manifesting the art of individuals who are labelled or identify as: invisibly disabled, neurodiverse, mentally ill, living with a DSM diagnosis, clients of mental health services or equivalent. we believe that most of Workman Arts' artist members would not accept pathologization, but use any of these etiquettes as a reclaim or for access to the necessary services.


this map is activated by DDADH, the dispossessed disabled artists demanding housing. most of DDADH members are artist members of WA.


this map shares DDADH's research and advocacy and gathers your housing crises and housing utopias.


the goal of this map is to convince Workman Arts to have a housing worker and long-term housing strategist on staff.


DDADH has already worked for two years on this goal and has made some progress, but there has been a confusing semi-responsiveness on WA's side, which has reminded us of how the city of Toronto undermines research and proposals by the people at the core of systemic discrimination.


still we choose to believe that WA would agree that "art" in its institutional definition is undue privilege if the basics such as housing are not covered.


Workman Arts: it is time to shift your priorities.


other organizations have made the shift and are taking responsibility in making sure that our human rights are respected. this is art and it is urgent and it yours to do with us.


our human right to housing is enshrined in the Canada National Housing Strategy Act since 2019.
